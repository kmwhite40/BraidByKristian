# Questions for Kristian — aftercare content

**What this is for.** Her booking site answers everything up to the appointment — deposits,
how to prep, what happens if you're late. It answers nothing about what happens *after*, and
that is what people search for: "how long do knotless braids last", "can I wash my braids",
"how to sleep with braids". Those searches are how new clients in Garland find a braider.

**Why we have to ask rather than write it.** Anything published here reads as *her* advice. If a
client follows aftercare guidance from her site and it damages their hair — or contradicts what
she actually tells people at the chair — that is a real problem, and she never said it. So none
of this gets written until she has answered in her own words. The rest of the site follows the
same rule: where she has not spoken, it stays silent.

**How to answer.** Voice notes are ideal — talk the way she'd talk to a client in the chair.
Rambling is fine, half-answers are fine, "it depends" is fine (and is often the honest answer
worth publishing). Skipping any question is fine.

**Priority.** ★ questions are the high-traffic ones. If she only has ten minutes, do those.

---

## 1. How long it lasts ★

The single most-searched question about every style she offers.

1. ★ How long should each style last before it needs taking down? Does it differ much between
   knotless, bohemian, twists, locs and cornrows?
2. ★ What makes braids last longer, and what makes them fail early?
3. At what point is someone leaving them in too long? What starts going wrong?
4. Is there a point where she'd rather someone came back sooner than they planned?

## 2. Washing and keeping the scalp clean ★

2. ★ Can clients wash their hair with braids in? How, and how often?
3. ★ What should they use — and is there anything she'd tell them to avoid outright?
4. How should they dry afterwards?
5. What about dry scalp, flaking or build-up between washes?

## 3. Sleeping and daily upkeep ★

6. ★ What should someone do with their hair at night? Scarf, bonnet, pillowcase?
7. ★ What does she recommend for daily upkeep — and how much is too much handling?
8. How should they refresh a style that's a few weeks old and starting to look fuzzy?
9. Anything she'd say about ponytails, buns, or tying braids up?

## 4. Tightness, itching and edges ★

This is where people get hurt, and where honest advice matters most.

10. ★ Some tightness after braiding is normal — where's the line between normal and "this is too
    tight, tell me"? What should someone do if it's painful?
11. ★ How does she protect edges, and what should clients do to look after theirs?
12. What causes itching, and what actually helps?
13. Are there people or hair types she'd steer away from certain styles altogether?
14. What are the signs someone should take a break from protective styling?

## 5. Take-down, swimming, gym, and the rest

15. Should clients take braids down themselves or come to her? Any way to do it badly?
16. How long should hair rest between styles?
17. Swimming — pool or ocean. Anything to do before and after?
18. Working out and sweating — does that change the routine?
19. Colour: anything to know about coloured braiding hair or colour-treated natural hair?
20. Kids' hair — anything she does differently, or wants parents to know?

## Two more, for the site rather than a guide

21. **Choosing a size.** Small vs medium vs large changes price *and* how long someone sits.
    How would she explain the difference, and how does she help someone choose? *(This would
    become a size guide — a real source of confusion and mis-booking today.)*

22. **A current bio.** Her published bio still says *Fate, TX*. It was removed rather than
    edited, so the About page has no bio at all right now. A few sentences in her own words —
    how she started, what she likes braiding, what she wants clients to feel — would fill the
    one visibly empty spot on the site.

---

## What happens to the answers

Each section becomes a page under a guides section, written from her words and quoting her
where the phrasing is better than anything we'd write. Nothing gets published that she hasn't
said, and she should read anything with her name on it before it goes live.

The size guide (21) and bio (22) go straight into the existing pages.

If she says "I don't really have a rule for that" — that is a genuine answer, and the honest
move is to leave the question unanswered rather than invent a rule for her.
